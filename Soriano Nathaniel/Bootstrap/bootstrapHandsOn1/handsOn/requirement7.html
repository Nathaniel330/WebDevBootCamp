Three equal columns

Three equal-width columns with space in-between at desktop view and scales to larger views. With browser windows smaller than desktop, these columns must stack.

code:

    <div class="text-center text-white">
        <div class="row d-flex justify-content-between">
            <div class="col-lg-3 bg-danger">col-lg-3</div>
            <div class="col-lg-3 bg-dark">col-lg-3</div>
            <div class="col-lg-3 bg-primary">col-lg-3</div>
        </div>
    </div>

result:

col-lg-3
col-lg-3
col-lg-3

Unequal columns

columns with different width. column sizes started from desktop, scaling to larger views. breaks below 992px.

code:

    <div class="text-center text-white">
        <div class="row">
            <div class="col-lg-3 bg-danger">col-lg-3</div>
            <div class="col-lg-9 bg-dark">col-lg-9</div>
        </div> 
        <div class="row">
            <div class="col-lg-6 bg-info">col-lg-6</div>
            <div class="col-lg-6 bg-primary">col-lg-6</div>
        </div>
        <div class="row">
            <div class="col bg-success">col</div>
        </div>
    </div>

result:

col-lg-3
col-lg-9
col-lg-6
col-lg-6
col

Two columns

two columns. different width. always within the same row in every browser window.

code:

    <div class="row text-light text-center">
        <div class="col-8 bg-dark">col-8</div>
        <div class="col-4 bg-success">col-4</div>
    </div>

result:

col-8
col-4

no grid classes necessary for full-width elements
